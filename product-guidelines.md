# Product Guidelines: Sales Language System

## Purpose

This file defines the enforceable sales-language rules for customer-facing copy.

Use it for:

- landing pages
- pricing
- registration
- checkout
- payment success
- member portal states
- partner-facing conversion copy

If a surface conflicts with this file, this file wins.

## Core Hierarchy

Use this order everywhere customer-facing copy appears:

1. `Membership` = the commercial offer
2. `IDA App` = the digital utility members use, when naming it improves clarity
3. `IDA Card` = the proof artifact inside the app

## Non-Negotiable Rule

We sell `membership` first.
We use `IDA App` to explain utility where useful.
We use `IDA Card` to prove activation and membership status.

Never invert this hierarchy on public conversion surfaces.

## Canonical Definitions

### Membership

Meaning:

- the paid relationship
- the annual commercial offer
- the thing the user buys

Required on:

- hero / cold landing
- pricing
- registration
- checkout
- payment confirmation
- renewal / cancellation
- plan comparison

### IDA App

Meaning:

- the digital workspace attached to membership
- where the member uses the service
- not the sole commercial object on cold conversion surfaces

Use when:

- it improves understanding of utility
- the user is learning how the service works
- the user has already paid
- the surface is educational, diaspora-facing, or institutional

### IDA Card

Meaning:

- proof of active membership
- digital ID / QR / wallet artifact
- activation proof
- not the product itself

Use when:

- proving activation
- showing wallet utility
- reassuring the user post-payment
- supporting partner-assisted selling

## Surface Rules

### Must be Membership-First

These surfaces must lead with `membership`, not `IDA App` or `IDA Card`:

- hero / cold landing
- pricing
- registration
- checkout
- payment confirmation

### Can Elevate IDA App More Directly

These surfaces may use `IDA App` more prominently:

- post-payment success
- onboarding
- member portal
- product education
- diaspora explainers
- institutional explainers

### Can Use IDA Card Tactically

These surfaces may use `IDA Card` more prominently as proof:

- post-payment proof moments
- wallet surfaces
- membership verification moments
- partner-assisted selling
- member dashboard proof panels

## Approved Core Phrases

### Offer

- `Start membership`
- `Activate membership`
- `Membership active`
- `Annual membership`
- `Your membership includes access to IDA App`

### Utility

- `Use IDA App for guidance, claim packs, tracking, and next steps`
- `Access IDA App after activation`
- `Open IDA App`
- `Continue in IDA App`

### Proof

- `Your IDA Card is ready inside the app`
- `View IDA Card`
- `IDA Card (proof of membership)`
- `Add your IDA Card to Apple Wallet`
- `Your IDA Card appears after activation`

## Banned Primary Framing

Do not use these as the main public selling line:

- `Get your digital card`
- `Buy the digital card`
- `Activate the card`
- `Digital card for €20/year`
- `Kartë digjitale` as the product name
- `IDA Card` as the hero-level product name
- `Activate IDA App` as the only cold-traffic buying frame

## Never Imply

- that the user is buying only a card
- that the app alone is the paid commercial object
- that membership is secondary to the artifact or interface

## Funnel Enforcement

### Hero / Cold Landing

Must be:

- outcome-led
- membership-first
- utility-second
- proof-third

Primary CTA must be membership-first.

### Pricing

Plans must be framed as membership plans.

### Registration

Registration must be framed as continuation toward membership.

### Checkout

Checkout must sell activation of membership, not app access alone.

### Post-Payment Success

Success must confirm membership first, then explain utility, then show proof.

### Member Portal

Portal may explain utility via `IDA App`, but membership status remains primary where status matters.

## Review Checks

Before approving any customer-facing copy, verify:

1. Does the surface clearly say what the user is buying?
2. Is `membership` the paid offer?
3. Is `IDA App` presented as utility, not the sole commercial object?
4. Is `IDA Card` clearly secondary proof, not the product?
5. Would a first-time user understand the relationship between all three in under 5 seconds?

If any answer is `no`, the copy is not approved.

## PR Review Shortcuts

Use these in copy/design reviews:

- `This copy weakens the membership-first offer.`
- `IDA App is being used as the commercial object here instead of utility framing.`
- `IDA Card is being elevated too far; it should stay proof, not product.`
- `The hierarchy is inverted on this surface.`
- `This step does not clearly tell the user what they are buying.`

## Final Reviewer Test

Ask this before approving:

`If I saw only this screen, would I know that I am buying a membership, using IDA App as the service utility where useful, and receiving IDA Card as proof?`

If the answer is not clearly `yes`, the copy is not ready.

## Golden Summary

We sell `membership` first, use `IDA App` to explain utility where useful, and use `IDA Card` to prove activation and membership status.
